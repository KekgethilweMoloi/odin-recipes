Wonton Soup

Homemade wonton soup

This homemade wonton soup is filled with juicy pork and prawn wontons, and a light broth.

It is easy to make, freeze and have later as a late night pick-me-up.

Ingredients

  • 50 to 60 wonton wrappers
Wonton Filling
  • 200 g pork mince, lean
  • 200 g prawns, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger, finely grated
  • 2 shallots / green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp chinese cooking wine
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil, toasted
Broth (For 2 Servings)
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed (6)
  • 1/3 of 1 cm piece ginger, sliced
  • 1 1/2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 1/2 tbsp chinese cooking wine
  • 1/4 to 1/2 tsp sesame oil
To Serve
  • Shallots/ scallions, finely chopped
  • Bok choy, quartered
  • 40 to 50 g dried egg noodles per person

Steps

Wontons
  1. Place Filling ingredients in a bowl.
  2. Use a potato masher to mash until fairly smooth – about 20 mashes.
  3. Do not turn the prawn into a complete paste, small chunks are good.
Wrapping
  1. Lay Wontons on work surface. Use 2 teaspoons to put the Filling on the wontons.
  2. Work in batches of 5 if starting out, up to 15 or 20 if confident.
  3. Brush 2 edges with water. Fold to seal, pressing out air.
  4. Brush water on one corner and bring corners together, pressing to seal.
  5. Place wrapped wontons into a container with a lid as you work (so they do not dry out).
Cooking/Freezing
    To cook:
  1. Bring a large pot of water to boil.
  2. Place wontons in water and cook for 4 minutes or until they float.
  3. Remove with slotted spoon straight into serving bowls. Ladle over broth.
    4. To freeze:
  4. Freeze uncooked in airtight containers.
  5. Cook from frozen for 6 to 8 minutes.
    6. IMPORTANT: Do not freeze if you made this with defrosted frozen prawns.
Broth
  1. Place Broth ingredients in a saucepan over high heat.
  2. Add white ends of scallions/shallots if leftover from Wonton filling.
  3. Place lid on, bring to simmer then reduce to medium high and simmer for 5 – 10 minutes to allow the flavours to infuse. Pick garlic and ginger out before using.
  4. If using vegetables, blanch in the soup broth and place in serving bowl.
Assemble Soup
  1. Prepare noodles according to packet directions (if using noodles).
  2. Place in serving bowl with cooked wontons and blanched vegetables.
  3. Ladle over soup. Serve!
